About Me

By day, I’ve been the architect behind products—shaping roadmaps, designing flows, herding features like they were caffeinated cats. But somewhere along the way, I fell in love with the gears beneath the UI: the logic, the syntax, the quiet elegance of things that work because someone thought them through in code. So now, like a plot twist I probably should’ve seen coming, I’m trading my product hat for a developer’s keyboard—building not just the “what” but the “how,” one semicolon at a time.
